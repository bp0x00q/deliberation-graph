# Criticism protocol

Critique the proposal rather than its author or writing style.

Use a specific attack class:

- hard-constraint violation;
- hidden dependency;
- incorrect or unfalsifiable assumption;
- operational, security, scaling, or migration failure;
- concrete counterexample;
- demonstrably simpler alternative;
- unsupported, circular, stale, or low-quality evidence.

A useful critique names the failure mechanism and the condition that triggers it. “This seems risky” is not sufficient.

The target branch gets one explicit response and revision pass. Record:

1. the critique;
2. whether it is answered or accepted;
3. the mitigation or change;
4. the weakness that remains.

A high score never cancels a failed hard gate or unresolved blocking critique.
